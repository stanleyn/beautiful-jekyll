--- layout: page use-site-title: false --- Hello. I am a postdoc at Stanford working in computational and systems immunology with Nima Aghaeepour. My research is broadly focused on 1) mass cytometry bioinformatics 2) single-cell analysis 3) computational immunology and 4) graph algorithms. Mass cytometry is an amazing technology that enables the simultaneous measurement of >45 proteins at a single-cell level. As a postdoc, I have focused on how to relate single-cell mass cytometry data to external variables of interest (such as clinical outcome). This is a challenging problem, given the heterogenity and diverse amount of information contained across the hundreds of millions of cells in an individual single-cell experiment. Prior to joining Stanford, I finished my PhD in 2018 at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill with Peter Mucha. My graduate research was focused on graph algorithms and in particular, community detection. I love collaborating with people with complementary skillsets and I always enjoy the opportunity to learn immunology and cell biology. Amy Tsai
