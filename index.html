I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Computer Science and Computational Medicine Program at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill. My research is broadly focused on 1) single-cell bioinformatics 2) computational and systems immunology, and 4) developng algorithms for graph-based data (a lot of which is very appropriate for single-cell methods!). Single Cell Mass cytometry in paticular is an amazing technology that enables the simultaneous measurement of >40 proteins at a single-cell level. As a postdoc, I have focused on how to relate single-cell mass cytometry data to external variables of interest (such as clinical outcome). This is a challenging problem, given the heterogenity and diverse amount of information contained across the hundreds of millions of cells in an individual single-cell experiment.<\p>

I completed training as a postdoc in computational and systems immunology at Stanford with Nima Aghaeepour from 2018-2020. Prior to joining Stanford, I finished my PhD in 2018 with Peter Mucha, where my research focused on graph partitioning.
