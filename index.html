--- layout: page title: Natalie Stanley subtitle: Postdoc at Stanford. use-site-title: true --- Hello. I am a postdoc at Stanford working in computational and systems immunology with Prof. Nima Aghaeepour. My current research interests are in mass cytometry bioinformatics and developing novel machine learning techniques for integrating data across multiple biological modalities (e.g. multiomics). I particularly like working with CyTOF data for its ability to reveal a holistic view of the immune system, along with its predictive power in clinical studies. Prior to joining Stanford, I finished my PhD at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill with Prof. Peter Mucha. My graduate research was focused on developing and extending community detection methods in networks. I focused on methods to accomodate more complex types of network structures, such as those that are multilayer (e.g. multiple relational definitions) and large. I am driven by learning immunology and collaborating with people who have complementary skill sets. I also love to talk about graph Laplacians ;).
