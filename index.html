--- layout: page title: Natalie Stanley subtitle: Postdoc at Stanford. use-site-title: true --- Hello. I am a postdoc at Stanford working in computational and systems immunology with Prof. Nima Aghaeepour. My current research interests are in flow/mass cytometry bioinformatics and developing novel machine learning approaches for integrating multiomics datasets. I particularly like working with CyTOF data and its ability to reveal a holistic view of the immune system and its predictive power in clinical studies. I am new to immunology, but feel like I have found my second passion (after machine learning :)) in life. This is a rich area of biology that is not well understood. Prior to joining Stanford, I finished my PhD in computational biology at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill with Dr. Peter Mucha. My graduate research was focused on developing and extending community detection approaches in networks. I focused on methods to accomodate more complex types of network structures, such as those that are multilayer (e.g. multiple relational definitions) and large. During this time I also fell in love with North Carolina and found my home in the world. :)
